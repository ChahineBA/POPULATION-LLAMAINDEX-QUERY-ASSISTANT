In Canada, English and French are the two official languages. Approximately 57% of Canadians speak English, 21% speak French, and 16% are bilingual in both languages. French is the only official language of Quebec, but there are also substantial French-speaking populations in New Brunswick, Alberta, and Manitoba, with Ontario having the largest French-speaking population outside Quebec. New Brunswick is the only officially bilingual province. There are also 11 Indigenous language groups, composed of more than 65 distinct languages and dialects. Inuktitut is the majority language in Nunavut and is one of three official languages in the territory. As of the 2021 census, just over 7.8 million Canadians listed a non-official language as their first language, with Mandarin, Punjabi, Cantonese, Spanish, Arabic, Tagalog, Italian, German, and Tamil being some of the most common.
